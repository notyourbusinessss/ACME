  • Falls on the Coaticook River

    ARTIST

    William S. Hunter, Jr. (1823-1894)

    CREATION DATE

    unknown (mid-nineteenth century)

    MEDIUM

    lithograph: ink on paper; 23.1 cm x 29.8 cm

    PROVENANCE

    Gift of Monique Cholette and Philip H. Scowen, before 1993.

    ASSOCIATED GENRES

    Canadian Art, Hudson Valley School, Landscape, Quebec Art, Realism, Travelogue / Expeditionary Art, Lithography

    DESCRIPTION

    Falls on the Coaticook River is an illustration of the Coaticook River cascading down rocky cliffs to the riverbed, emphasizing the grandiosity and power of nature associated with the aesthetic sublime, a trend popularized by Edmund Burke in his 1757 Philosophical Enquiry into the Origin of our Ideas of the Sublime and Beautiful. Here, Hunter utilizes scale of proportionally dwarfed trees and felled branches to communicate the vastness and power of the falls.
